About Me

Hello! My name is Charlie Barklam, and I'm a 20-year-old second-year Computing Student from London, currently studying at Imperial College London, with a strong background in looking dope as hell, being pretty cool, and general tomfoolery.

I am particularly interested in low-level programming and the development of properly cool shit. ChatGPT is cool but can it SegFault like I can? Don't think so!

Alongside my studies, I have fiery passions in music, drama, and especially performing them (give me that attention!). I cover one whole instrument of the orchestra, or three if you add a shouting piano player. I've also starred in a few dramatic performances in my years (the real ones would have been to watch).

You'll also catch me getting rinsed on the football pitch, but I once scored 20 goals in a Flower Pot season.

I originally had very little experience with HTML, CSS, JavaScript and standard frameworks like Flask, but these projects have helped me to build those skills to a professional level.

Some of my proudest moments have come from my groupwork, some of which you can view on my GitHub.

Comparatively, front-end work is quite new to me, so in light of this flaw that was in my skillset, I decided to write and manage the Imperial College Musical Theatre Society's website, along with this one.

My largest project to date is building an AArch64 Emulator and Assembler: Myself and my team developed a 64-bit ARMv8 toolchain featuring an emulator and a two-pass assembler, which allowed users to write and execute ARMv8 assembly source code, safe on any hardware and OS, thanks to a fully portable and endian-safe design. This was done entirely in C, allowing me to develop a mastery with C and core low level computing concepts.

More interestingly, I also embarked on a journey to create a real-time loop-based music peformance platform, once again in C, featuring track selection, mixing, live playback, with scheduling and customisation tools to allow for music of different keys and speeds.
